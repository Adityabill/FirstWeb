It is the capital city of the Indian state of West Bengal. It is also known as the "City Of Joy". It is the financial hub of East India and it is the 3rd populous and the 3rd richest metropolis of India, after Delhi and Mumbai.

*Things to see in Kolkata

  • Victoria Memorial:

    • It is a large marble building, which was built between 1906 and 1921. It was built in the memory of Queen Victoria, Empress Of India. It is now a museum and a popular tourist destination in the city. It open from 10:00 am to 5:00 pm everyday except Mondays and national holidays.

    Fig. Victoria Memorial

  • Howrah Bridge:

    • It is bridge with suspension span over the Hoogly river, connecting Kolkata with the industrial city, Howrah. The bridge was commissioned in 1943. In 1965, the bridge was renamed as Rabindranath Setu, after the great Bengali poet Rabindranath Tagore. It is currently the six longest bridge of its type in the world.

    Fig. Howrah Bridge (Source:Google Images)

  • Vidyasagar Setu:

    • Commonly known as the second Howrah Bridge, it is a toll bridge over Hooghly river, connecting Kolkata with Howrah. The bridge is named after a former educationalist reformer, Pandit Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar and was commissioned in 1992. It is the longest cable-stayed bridge in India.

    Fig. Vidyasagar Setu(Source:Google Images)

  • Eco Park:

    • It is an urban park, located in the IT hub of Rajarhat Newtown. The park exhibits the replicas of the Seven Wonders of the World. The place is best for romantic and family hangouts.

    Fig. Eco Park

  • Indian Museum:

    • It is the oldest and the largest museum in India established in 1814 by the Asiatic Society Of Bengal. It exhibits some of the rare collections of antiques, ornaments, fossils, skeletons, mummies, Mughal Paintings.

    Fig. Indian Museum(Source: Google Images)

*How To Reach

  1. 1.Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport is the primary airport of the city. It has direct flights to all the metro cities of India and also to many other major international cities like Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Singapore etc.
  2. 2.Howrah Railway Junction and Sealdah Railway Junction are the major stations in the city providing trains to many parts of India.
  3. 3.Kolkata is well connected to many parts of india by roads as well. Many speedy expressways like 'Golden Quadrilateral' run through the city.
  4. 4.The Hooghly river serves as the Inland Waterway 1. It provides transportion to many cities along the Ganga river like Benaras and Allahabad in Uttar Pradesh.
